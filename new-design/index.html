We are a Church Planting Network dedicated to fostering a new kind of church in post-Christendom contexts.

The Post-Christendom Challenge

Mission Anabaino emerged from a thirty-plus year post-Christendom context in North America, specifically New England - arguably the "least religious region of North America." In this environment, our deepest convictions are often not acknowledged or respected. The words "God" and "Church" are frequently treated as relics of the past.

Yes, like other post-Christendom places, our birthing grounds of New England are desperate for more churches. But even more so, post-Christendom places need a "new kind" of church! This has become MA's area of expertise. Over the years, it was discovered that the Christendom-model of planting churches most common in our denominational contexts was just not adequate. It often led to disillusionment and false expectations for our church planters. It also failed to reflect a strategy suitable to truly "scratch" plants.

Our Response

We recognize the need for not just more churches, but a "new kind" of church that can effectively engage with and minister to a post-Christendom society. We aim to rediscover the sacred first principles of the apostolic missionary church. We translate these principles into social liturgies that address post-modernity challenges. We reconstruct the pre-modern, apostolic church for a post-Christendom context.

More than a network

Comprehensive Support

We are a comprehensive planter-support community. Developing theological vision for the long-haul of planting and leading a church. Cultivating and allocating financial and other fiscal resources, going beyond typical network activities.

Post-Christendom Expertise

Rather than just "rethinking" strategy, forms and so called "philosophy of ministry," we believe our task is to do the much hard work of first deconstructing the Christendom-modernist "church" such as to then reconstruct the pre-modern, apostolic church in a way that can faithfully be translated into a post-Christendom context.

Theological Collaborative

Our theological collaborative is focused on re-discovering the nature of the church by exploring the relationship between Christ as our covenantal head and our temple presence in the local church. We are committed to our Total Christ, Five Marks vision in missional ecclesiology, which guides our understanding and practice of planting and leading churches in a post-Christendom context.

Our Story

It involves participating in the real and personal advent of Christ through church planting and the ecumenically-oriented rediscovery of missional ecclesiology.
