Welcome to Mission Anabaino

Since you found us then you already know we are a Church Planting Network.

Yes, we are invested in planting churches by

  • Recruiting, assessing and developing Church planting apprenticeships,
  • Encouraging, coaching and supporting church planters,
  • Fostering a church planting culture both in our MA affiliated anchor churches and the churches we plant,
  • Developing theological vision for the long-haul of planting and leading a church.
  • Cultivating and allocating financial and other fiscal resources.

...Bla Bla Bla

Ok, if you’re a little cynical about church planting networks you are not alone. While we believe that network activities are good and essential aspects of church planting, did you know that we are also a comprehensive planter-support community?

MA was generated out of a thirty plus year post-Christendom context in North America that is arguably the “least religious region of North America.”[2] It is a place where, in the words of Stefan Pass, “ our deepest convictions were not acknowledged at all, or even respected... and we begin to realize how completely self-evident it is for the vast majority of those we encounter to live without God or the Church.” However much the words “God” and “Church” are still present, it was more often than not words relating to antiques or rare books.[3]

Yes, like other post-Christendom places, our birthing grounds of New England are desperate for more churches. But even more so, post-Christendom places need a “new kind” of church! This has become MA’s area of expertise. Over the years, it was discovered that the Christendom-model of planting churches most common in our denominational contexts was just not adequate. It often led to disillusionment and false expectations for our church planters. It also failed to reflect a strategy suitable to truly “scratch” plants.

How then do we BOTH– rediscover the sacred first principles of the apostolic missionary church (forgotten in traditional Christendom) AND translate those into social liturgies and forms that speak to the circumstances and challenges of post-modernity? Rather than just “rethinking” strategy, forms and so called “philosophy of ministry,” we believe our task is to do the the much hard work of first deconstructing the Christendom-modernist “church” such as to then reconstruct the pre-modern, apostolic church in a way that can faithfully be translated into a post-Christendom context.” And we do all this while fostering a “band of brothers” support network that sustains us in our sacred calling together!

In summary, MA wants to plant churches

But we also want to be an active collaborative in the re-discovery of WHAT the church IS by nature in relation to Christ as both our covenantal head and our temple presence in and through the local church! We call this our Total Christ, Five Marks vision in missional ecclesiology. To avoid the customary pendulum swings of “either-or” church strategy, we seek to answer two fundamental questions. First, what in redemptive history was always present and fulfilled by Christ as an essential element of the gospel and foundational to the missionary church? Second, and the all-important question in missional ecclesiology, how do these essential elements get formed during Christ’s ascension ministry as mediated in the “Body of Christ?”

We hope this brief explanation of MA has encouraged you to read more about our Theological “Total Christ” vision and what we believe are the “Five Foundational Marks that become the metrics of a missionary local church.” It is a theological vision that we pray will bring substance to Lesslie Newbigin’s great declaration concerning the missionary nature of the church when he writes: The church is not the source of witness, it is the locus of witness.... The presence of a new reality, the presence in the shared life of the church of the spirit...[4]
