The Post-Christendom Challenge

Mission Anabaino emerged from a thirty-plus year post-Christendom context in North America, specifically New England - arguably the "least religious region of North America." In this environment, our deepest convictions are often not acknowledged or respected. The words "God" and "Church" are frequently treated as relics of the past.

The Need for a New Kind of Church

Yes, like other post-Christendom places, our birthing grounds of New England are desperate for more churches. But even more so, post-Christendom places need a "new kind" of church! This has become MA's area of expertise. Over the years, it was discovered that the Christendom-model of planting churches most common in our denominational contexts was just not adequate. It often led to disillusionment and false expectations for our church planters. It also failed to reflect a strategy suitable to truly "scratch" plants.

Our Response

We recognize the need for not just more churches, but a "new kind" of church that can effectively engage with and minister to a post-Christendom society. Our approach involves:

  • Rediscovering the sacred first principles of the apostolic missionary church
  • Translating these principles into social liturgies that address post-modernity challenges
  • Reconstructing the pre-modern, apostolic church for a post-Christendom context

Key Challenges in Post-Christendom

1. Secularization

The increasing separation of religion from public life and institutions.

2. Loss of Religious Literacy

Declining knowledge and understanding of religious concepts and traditions.

3. Individualism

The emphasis on personal choice over communal religious identity.

4. Pluralism

The coexistence of multiple religious and non-religious worldviews.

Our Strategy

Mission Anabaino addresses these challenges through:

  • Developing contextually appropriate church planting models
  • Training leaders in post-Christendom ministry approaches
  • Fostering communities that engage meaningfully with contemporary culture
  • Emphasizing the relevance of the gospel in a pluralistic society
